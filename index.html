Little Bits of Background

In grade school, I created images on the computer using lines and pixels. The teacher asked me what website I got that from, and how I got in onto the computer. Oh, the frustration. But of course, I cannot be stopped.

As a teenager, I attended the Academy of Art University one summer for non-credit classes learning about video game design and 3D modeling. What a fantastic summer.

As an adult, I attended Joliet Junior College where I earned my associate in arts. I then transferred to NIU where I graduated with a bachelors in nutrition. I changed my major a few times trying to figure out where the path of greatest success would be. I didn't want to be a starving artist, and my parents were big on veganism at the time so I thought I would become a nutritionist, since food is medicine. I realized close to my graduation that I didn’t want to put feeding tubes in people, or tell people what to eat or how to live their life.

Around that same time, (2017) I started creating art to sell at events for extra money. I created an etsy shop to reach the online community and started selling my art at events all around America. Selling art is a valuable source of income, but it can be unpredictable and unstable.

I am looking for something I can love. I have recently had a lot of time to sit and think. I remember taking a programming class in highschool and how much I loved it, but being shied away by it being a male-dominated field. High School was rough, and the last thing I wanted was to be seen as more “nerdier” than I already felt/was.

Present Day - I have been teaching myself front-end development for the last 6 months through several online courses and personal curiosity. I have done the full dive. I have gotten lost in a Javascript framework. I have spent many hours finding every cool thing I can find with CSS. Flexbox, grid, animations. I have been wrestling with Javascript for a few months, and I feel confident I know the basics.
